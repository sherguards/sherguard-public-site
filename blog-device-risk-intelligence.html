What Is Device Risk Intelligence? A Complete Guide

Device risk intelligence helps businesses identify suspicious devices, automated environments, risky browsers, bot activity, and account abuse before fraud impacts customers, systems, or revenue.

Overview

Understanding device risk in modern online businesses

Every signup, login, API request, and payment originates from a device. Device risk intelligence analyzes these devices to determine whether activity appears legitimate or suspicious.

Fraudsters frequently use automation tools, virtual machines, headless browsers, browser farms, and manipulated environments to create accounts, abuse APIs, and bypass security controls.

By analyzing device signals, businesses can reduce fraud, improve trust decisions, and protect customer accounts.

Key Concepts

What device risk intelligence can detect

Suspicious Browsers

Detect browsers that exhibit unusual behavior, modified configurations, or automation indicators.

Headless Environments

Identify browsers running without a visible interface, often used in automation and bot attacks.

Automation Frameworks

Detect signals associated with Selenium, Puppeteer, Playwright, WebDriver, and other automation tools.

Account Abuse

Link suspicious devices to signup abuse, account takeover attempts, and fraudulent activity.

Bot Activity

Combine device signals with behavioral analysis to identify non-human activity.

Risk Scoring

Convert device observations into actionable trust scores and security decisions.
